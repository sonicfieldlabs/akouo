# /litany

## Purpose

`/litany` audits audiovisual and sonic analysis for simplistic binaries, especially claims that idealize sound against vision, writing, media, or technology.

It functions as an anti-mystification command.

## When To Use

Use `/litany` for:

- audiovisual work
- film, video, and installation analysis
- media theory
- critiques of sonic mysticism
- claims about sound as immersion, body, presence, authenticity, or liberation
- claims that oppose sound and vision too neatly
- checking a draft analysis for audiovisual litany patterns

## Skills Called

- `critical-political-listening`
- `transductive-media-listening`
- `acoulogical-object-listening` when needed to reground the critique in the sound object

## Execution Order

1. Run `critical-political-listening` to detect universalist, romantic, or binary assumptions.
2. Run `transductive-media-listening` to identify media, platform, apparatus, and conversion conditions.
3. Run `acoulogical-object-listening` when the critique needs to return to the perceptual sound object.
4. Produce an audit of litany patterns, anti-litany corrections, and remaining risks.

## Expected Output

Use `schemas/command-output.schema.json`.

The output must identify whether the analysis reproduces binaries such as:

- sound is immersive, vision is distant
- sound is body, image is intellect
- sound is presence, writing is absence
- sound is South, vision is North
- sound is authentic, visuality is artificial

## Guardrails

- Critique must be concrete and text-specific or object-specific.
- Do not reject embodiment, immersion, or presence automatically. Audit overstatement.
- Name the mediation that complicates the binary.
- Return to the sound object when critique becomes abstract.

## Must Not Do

- Do not replace sonic mysticism with automatic anti-sound claims.
- Do not accuse without showing the specific binary or mediation.
- Do not flatten audiovisual relations into a new universal formula.
- Do not ignore technical apparatus, playback, platform, interface, or archive.
- Do not confuse a useful distinction with a simplistic binary.

## Recommended Follow-Up

Recommend `/study` for broader theoretical development or `/reference` for concepts around audiovisuality, mediation, sound studies, and critique.
