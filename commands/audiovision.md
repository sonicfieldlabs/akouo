# /audiovision

## Purpose

`/audiovision` is the sound-image-scene command. It analyzes how sound, image, text, interface, timing, captioning, synchronization, and audiovisual phrasing co-produce a scene.

## When To Use

Use `/audiovision` for:

- video, film, trailers, games, animation, and installation
- subtitles, captions, UI sound, notification sound, or screen-based sonic events
- synchronization, offscreen sound, diegesis, added value, sound-image causality, or audiovisual phrasing
- critiques of sound-versus-vision claims
- agentic workflows that route between audio, image, caption, and interface evidence

## Skills Called

- `audiovisual-scenic-listening`
- `acoulogical-object-listening`
- `voice-speech-listening` when speech, subtitles, captions, or dialogue matter
- `critical-political-listening` when audiovisual claims risk mystification, exclusion, surveillance, identity overreach, or cultural flattening

## Execution Order

1. Run `audiovisual-scenic-listening` to map sound-image-text-scene relations and synchronization limits.
2. Run `acoulogical-object-listening` to reground the sound object before assigning visual source or meaning.
3. Run `voice-speech-listening` when speech, captions, subtitles, dialogue, ASR, or dubbing are present.
4. Run `critical-political-listening` when the report needs to audit sound-versus-vision binaries, access, platform, identity, or institutional stakes.
5. Synthesize without treating synchronization as proof of real-world causality.

## Expected Output

Use `schemas/command-output.schema.json`.

The output must include:

- sound-image-text relations
- evidence available for audio, image, caption, and interface claims
- synchronization and source-causality limits
- scene-level interpretation
- access and caption cautions when relevant
- recommended next mode

## Guardrails

- Sound and image are co-produced through apparatus, editing, interface, platform, and playback.
- Do not idealize sound as automatically more embodied, authentic, immersive, or present than image.
- Do not invent visual content from audio-only input.
- Do not invent sonic content from visual-only input.
- Captions and subtitles are scene evidence, access paths, and mediations, not transparent replacements for sound.

## Must Not Do

- Do not turn synchronization into proof of source.
- Do not collapse audiovisual scenes into audio-only analysis.
- Do not repeat simplistic sound-versus-vision binaries.
- Do not ignore captions, subtitles, interface design, or access paths.
- Do not identify source, intention, edit history, or production chain without evidence.

## Recommended Follow-Up

Recommend `/litany` for a deeper audit of sound-versus-vision claims. Recommend `/voice` when speech, captions, dubbing, or voice agents dominate. Recommend `/access` when the scene depends on captions, warnings, or alternate sensory paths.
