# /fiction

## Purpose

`/fiction` is the speculative sonic world command. It develops declared symbolic, mythic, ritual, dreamlike, alien, or possible-world readings while preserving boundaries between evidence and imagination.

## When To Use

Use `/fiction` for:

- sonic fiction
- sound design worldbuilding
- mythic, ritual, or dreamlike sound prompts
- alien voice or synthetic entities
- game, film, installation, or narrative sound worlds
- hyperstitional or speculative audio concepts
- AI-generated sonic descriptions that should be treated imaginatively, not evidentially

## Skills Called

- `akouo-router` as an implicit planning pass that supplies the evidence inventory, risks, and forbidden assumptions the synthesis must respect
- `symbolic-fictional-listening`
- `embodied-affective-listening`
- `ecological-posthuman-listening` when nonhuman, habitat, elemental, or cosmological relations matter
- `critical-political-listening` as corrective when real cultures, histories, identities, archives, violence, or sacred forms are involved

## Execution Order

1. Run `symbolic-fictional-listening` to establish the speculative frame.
2. Run `embodied-affective-listening` to identify bodily force, atmosphere, pressure, trance, dread, pleasure, or fatigue.
3. Run `ecological-posthuman-listening` when the fiction includes nonhuman agencies, habitats, cosmic scales, weather, machines, or elemental relations.
4. Run `critical-political-listening` when the fiction risks appropriation, flattening, extraction, or confusion with real evidence.
5. Synthesize as declared sonic fiction with clearly separated claim categories.

## Expected Output

Use `schemas/command-output.schema.json`.

The output must include:

- speculative frame
- perceptual or prompt anchors
- symbolic registers
- implied world, agents, forces, atmosphere, and temporal logic
- risks and cautions
- what remains evidence, inference, interpretation, speculation, or unknown

## Guardrails

- Speculation must be labeled.
- Fiction must not become evidence.
- Use critique when fiction touches real peoples, archives, rituals, violence, territories, or identities.
- Keep perceptual anchors visible so worldbuilding does not float away from the sound.

## Must Not Do

- Do not present invented worlds as real events.
- Do not turn forensic, archival, or testimonial material into aesthetic fantasy by default.
- Do not appropriate sacred or culturally specific material casually.
- Do not hide hallucination inside poetic confidence.
- Do not ignore `undetermined` just because the command is creative.

## Recommended Follow-Up

Recommend `/reference` for sonic fiction, myth, cosmology, or speculative method routes. Recommend `/listen` or `/full-ear` if the user needs grounded analysis before fiction.
