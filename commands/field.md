# /field

## Purpose

`/field` is the field recording and situated listening command. It distinguishes soundscape, acoustemology, aurality, sensor mediation, fieldwork ethics, place, habitat, infrastructure, and material event.

## When To Use

Use `/field` for:

- field recordings, soundwalks, environmental recordings, and site notes
- forests, rivers, oceans, weather, animals, insects, birds, urban habitats, infrastructures, and atmospheres
- hydrophones, contact microphones, geophones, ambisonics, and remote listening
- soundscape, acoustemology, aurality, listening-with, and fieldwork method questions
- ecological or more-than-human agent workflows

## Skills Called

- `ecological-posthuman-listening`
- `transductive-media-listening`
- `critical-political-listening`
- `material-event-listening` when vibration, resonance, duration, feedback, infrastructure, or material process matters

## Execution Order

1. Run `ecological-posthuman-listening` to map human, nonhuman, environmental, weather, habitat, and infrastructural layers.
2. Run `transductive-media-listening` to map microphone, sensor, recorder, codec, platform, and elsewhere-listening mediation.
3. Run `critical-political-listening` to audit fieldwork ethics, extraction, access, territory, labor, coloniality, and infrastructural stakes.
4. Run `material-event-listening` when the sound is shaped by vibration, resonance, surfaces, propagation, duration, or low-frequency force.
5. Synthesize without romanticizing place as pure nature or treating field recording as direct access.

## Expected Output

Use `schemas/command-output.schema.json`.

The output must include:

- ecological layers and unavailable ecological facts
- fieldwork and capture mediation
- soundscape, acoustemology, or aurality distinctions when relevant
- site, season, species, and recorder-position unknowns
- political and access cautions
- recommended next mode

## Guardrails

- A field recording is not unmediated place.
- Do not identify species, location, season, weather, social context, or ecological condition without evidence.
- Do not romanticize nature or erase infrastructure.
- Do not treat acoustemology as the same thing as soundscape.
- Do not treat aurality as only individual listening preference; it can mark historical and social regimes of listening.

## Must Not Do

- Do not infer biodiversity, wilderness, indigeneity, territory, or ecological health from texture alone.
- Do not hide the recorder, microphone, platform, or listening position.
- Do not extract place into ambience without ethical caution.
- Do not treat nonhuman sound as symbolic material only.
- Do not ignore access, fieldwork permissions, or local knowledge when relevant.

## Recommended Follow-Up

Recommend `/tech` for signal or metadata inspection. Recommend `/method` for fieldwork design and sonic methodology. Recommend `/reference` for concepts such as soundscape, acoustemology, aurality, listening-with, or transduction.
